It may appear alien to many that traditional milk collectors and sellers, known as Goalas, used to visit households with their milk containers every morning to sell fresh milk. And households used to buy from them.Over the last two to three decades, things have changed a lot. Several industrial processors have emerged—collecting, processing and selling milk and milk products in packaged form with the promise of hygiene and quality. And Goalas have started losing their prominence.
